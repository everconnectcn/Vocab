lax
lax [lax laxer laxest] BrE [læks]  NAmE [læks]  adjective
1. (disapproving) not strict, severe or careful enough about work, rules or standards of behaviour
Syn: ↑slack, Syn: ↑careless
• lax security/discipline
• a lax attitude to health and safety regulations
2. (phonetics) (of a speech sound) produced with the muscles of the speech organs relaxed
Opp: ↑tense
Derived Word: ↑laxity
 
Word Origin:
late Middle English (in the sense ‘loose’, relating to the bowels): from Latin laxus.
