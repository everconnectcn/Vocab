cerebral
cere·bral BrE [ˈserəbrəl]  NAmE [səˈriːbrəl]  adjective
1. relating to the brain
• a cerebral haemorrhage
2. (formal) relating to the mind rather than the feelings
Syn: ↑intellectual
• His poetry is very cerebral.
 
Word Origin:
[cerebral] early 19th cent.: from Latin cerebrum ‘brain’ + ↑-al.

cerebral
cer‧e‧bral /ˈserəbrəl, ˈserɪbrəl $ səˈriː-, ˈserə-/ BrE  AmE  adjective
[Date: 1800-1900; Language: French; Origin: cérébral, from Latin cerebrum; CEREBRUM]
1. [only before noun] medical relating to or affecting your brain:
 a cerebral haemorrhage (=bleeding in the brain)
2. having or involving complicated ideas rather than strong emotions:
 a cerebral film
