perjury
per‧ju‧ry /ˈpɜːdʒəri $ ˈpɜːr-/ BrE  AmE  noun [uncountable] law
the crime of telling a lie after promising to tell the truth in a court of law, or a lie told in this way:
 Hall was found guilty of perjury.
